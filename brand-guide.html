01 / About

About Duininck Companies

A fourth-generation, family-owned portfolio spanning water management, construction, materials, golf course design, and real estate development. Celebrating 100 years in 2026.

For 100 years, the Duininck family has been building things that endure: roads, water systems, golf courses, communities, and a company culture rooted in faith and service. Now in its fourth generation, Duininck Companies proves that when you build on the right foundation, growth is not just possible but inevitable.
Mission
We are energized to build for the future with a focus on people, values, and growth.
Vision
To steward a thriving, multi-generational family business that advances its people, partners, and communities through values-driven growth.
Founding Story
In 1919, 16-year-old Henry Duininck began working on road construction in Minnesota. By 1926, he and his brothers Amos and Wilbur formalized Duininck Brothers Construction. A 1932 bankruptcy during the Depression forced a reorganization, but the family rebuilt. Four generations later, the company spans water management, construction, materials, golf course design, and real estate development.

06 / Architecture

Brand Architecture

How the parent brand and subsidiaries relate. Confirmed by CMO

"When a Duininck truck pulls up on a site, we need to represent ALL of it, not just a piece of it." /Nicole Behne, CMO. This quote defined the architecture decision: one unified Duininck brand for construction, with Golf retaining niche positioning.
Unified Duininck Brand Active
All construction and materials entities operating under a single "Duininck" brand. Concrete brand eliminated. Minnesota and Texas unified. One truck = all capabilities.
Niche Exception: Golf
Duininck Golf retains distinct positioning. Different audience (architects vs. GCs), different website, different content strategy. Endorsed as "A Duininck Company."
Independent Brands
Prinsco (water management) and Hart Ranch (real estate/golf) maintain independent brand identities. Different markets, different customers, established equity.

11 / Usage

Usage Guidelines

Rules for using the Duininck brand consistently.

Name Usage
Full name: "Duininck Companies" (for corporate communications, formal contexts)
Short name: "Duininck" (acceptable in running text after first full mention)
Never: "The Duininck Company" / "Duininck Inc." / "Duininck Corp."
Endorsement: "A Duininck Company" (for subsidiary use /especially Golf)
Centennial: "100 Years of Building Things That Endure" (2026 campaign line)
Content Guidelines (Post-Discovery)
No talking heads: Blue-collar workforce won't go on camera. Use cinematic b-roll with voiceover instead.
Film the work: Equipment, job sites, drone footage. The work IS the content.
Carousels over graphics: Static branded graphics get "two likes." Project story carousels perform.
Capture timing: Golf beauty shots come a YEAR after construction. Plan captures now for next year's content.
Voice differentiation: Golf voice is distinct from construction. Architects vs. general contractors.
Logo Guidelines
Brand unification in progress. Concrete brand logo being retired. Logo files, clear space rules, minimum sizes, and approved color variations will be finalized with the unified brand launch.
