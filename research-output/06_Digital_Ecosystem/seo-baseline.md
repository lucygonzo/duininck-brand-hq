## Heritage Competitor Discovery: Bolander

**New finding from SEO research:** Bolander (bolander.com) ranks for "heavy civil construction" in Minnesota and was **founded in 1924**, making them 102 years old. This is the SECOND heritage-comparable competitor discovered (after Ulland Brothers, founded 1920).

Three MN heavy civil companies now have centenarian heritage claims:
1. Ulland Brothers: 1920 (106 years)
2. Bolander: 1924 (102 years)
3. Duininck: 1926 (100 years)

This further reinforces that "100 years" alone is not a unique differentiator. The combination of years + generations + portfolio breadth + values depth is the wedge.

**Key Finding:** AI models pull from structured data sources (Wikipedia, industry directories, news coverage, detailed website content). Duininck is likely invisible to AI because:
1. No Wikipedia entry
2. Thin website content (blog publishes twice a year)
3. No structured case study pages with project details
4. No schema markup (TBD verification)
5. Limited press coverage in major trade publications
6. No ENR ranking (or not prominently featured)

### GEO Action Priorities
1. **Wikipedia/Wikidata entry**: Create a notable company article (100-year history, multiple subsidiaries, centennial event). This is the single highest-impact GEO action.
2. **Schema markup**: Add Organization, LocalBusiness, and Project schemas to all sites.
3. **Detailed case study pages**: Each major project needs its own URL with specs, dates, architect names, and outcomes. This is what AI models cite.
4. **Industry directory presence**: Ensure accurate listings on AGC, NAPA, GCBAA, ENR databases.
5. **Content volume**: Twice-per-year blog publishing is invisible. Need 2-4x monthly at minimum.
