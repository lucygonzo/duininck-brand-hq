## What They Post (Content Categories)

### 1. Employee Spotlights
Knife River regularly features individual employees with photos and quotes. Not talking heads on video, just still photos with caption-based stories. This works for blue-collar audiences who won't go on camera, exactly the constraint Duininck faces.

### 2. Values-Based Content
Posts tied to their four core values: People, Safety, Quality, Environment. Each post connects a real employee or project to a value. Feels authentic, not corporate.

### 3. Recruitment Events
Career fair participation, school visits (e.g., Jefferson High School employability skills assemblies). They're building pipeline visibility with high schoolers. This is the trade school pipeline play Duininck should be making at Ridgewater College.

### 4. Diversity Celebrations
Women in Construction Week content, celebrating contributions of women in shaping the construction industry. This gives them progressive employer brand positioning without being forced.

### 5. Team Events & Culture
Regional kickoff celebrations, employee appreciation events. Shows the human side of a 5,700-person operation.

## The Handle Itself: @lifeatknife

The handle IS the strategy. "Life at Knife" positions the Instagram as an employee experience channel, not a corporate marketing channel. This framing:
- Gives permission to post casual, authentic content
- Attracts followers who are prospective employees (the people you want to reach)
- Differentiates from corporate construction Instagram accounts that only post project photos
- Creates a "brand within a brand" specifically for employer brand

### What Duininck Could Learn
Duininck's @duininckinc is a generic corporate handle. If they created something like @lifeatduininck or @buildwithduininck, it reframes the channel as an invitation to join the culture, not just watch from the outside.

## Content Patterns That Work for Blue-Collar Audiences

Based on Knife River's approach:

1. **Still photos with caption stories** (not video interviews). The worker's face is in the photo, but the story is told in the caption. This works when workers are willing to be photographed but not filmed.
2. **Equipment as hero shots** with the operator credited in the caption. "Meet [Name], operating the [machine] at [project]." The equipment is the visual, the person is the story.
3. **Group photos at events** (kickoffs, safety milestones, appreciation days). These are low-effort captures that employees themselves share.
4. **School/career fair photos** showing Knife River representatives engaging with young people. Signals investment in the next generation.
5. **Seasonal content** tied to construction calendar (spring mobilization, summer peak, fall wrap-up).

## Implications for Duininck

Knife River has 2,385 followers with 696 posts. Duininck has 348 followers with 416 posts. Knife River's engagement per post is likely higher because their content is employee-focused (employees share and tag posts about themselves). Duininck's content mix is TBD but likely skews toward project/corporate content.

The gap isn't just follower count. It's strategic framing. Knife River built a channel FOR employees and recruits. Duininck built a channel ABOUT the company. The former generates organic sharing. The latter doesn't.
