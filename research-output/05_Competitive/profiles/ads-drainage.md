## Key Finding

ADS is a fundamentally different competitor than what Duininck faces in construction or golf. This is a product company competing on distribution, specs, price, and dealer relationships. The Duininck family story is unlikely to be a primary differentiator in pipe purchasing decisions.

### Where Prinsco Can Compete
1. **Regional strength**: Prinsco dominates in upper Midwest agricultural markets. Dealer relationships and local presence matter more than national brand.
2. **Technical specialization**: If Prinsco offers products or specs that ADS doesn't optimize for (ag drainage specifically), that's the wedge.
3. **Service and responsiveness**: A family-owned company can make decisions faster than a NYSE-listed corporation. Custom solutions, rapid response, and personal relationships.

### Recommendation
Prinsco's competitive strategy is a separate workstream from the Duininck brand unification. The Duininck brand story adds trust ("family-owned, 100 years") but doesn't drive pipe purchasing decisions. Focus brand resources on segments where the story IS the differentiator.
