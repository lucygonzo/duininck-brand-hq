## Executive Summary

Duininck Companies is a 4th-generation, family-owned portfolio headquartered in Willmar, Minnesota, operating since 1926. The portfolio spans three industry sectors: Water Management (Prinsco), Construction & Materials (Duininck Heavy Civil, Duininck Concrete), and Real Estate (Hart Ranch, Duininck Development). They employ 700+ people across 5+ states (MN, SD, TX, GA, and others), with ~90% field workers in blue-collar roles.

The company is entering its centennial year with a brand unification initiative led by CMO Nicole Behne (ex-Hormel, ex-Polaris, joined May 2025). The core strategic move: consolidating all construction entities under a single "Duininck" brand, eliminating the separate Concrete brand, while keeping Golf as a niche and Prinsco fully independent.

From the outside, the Duininck story is largely invisible. The centennial receives no prominent mention on the parent website. Prinsco and Hart Ranch show zero visible connection to the parent company. Six web properties use six different visual treatments. The richest brand story in Minnesota construction is being told to almost no one.

## Corporate Structure

### State Filings Research
Entity registrations found across Minnesota, South Dakota, and Texas Secretary of State filings.

The corporate evolution tells a diversification story:
- **1926**: Duininck Brothers Construction (original entity)
- **1975**: Prinsburg Tile Company founded (precursor to Prinsco)
- **1984**: Hart Ranch established in South Dakota
- **1986**: Prinsburg Tile becomes Prinsco, pivots to plastic pipe manufacturing
- **1988**: Golf division established
- **2004**: Acquired Central Allied Company (becomes Duininck Concrete)
- **2014**: Central Team created for shared enterprise services
- **2022**: Portfolio restructured into 3 industry sectors

### Subsidiaries

| Entity | Sector | HQ | Status | Brand Note |
|--------|--------|-----|--------|------------|
| Prinsco | Water Management | Willmar, MN | Flagship | Independent brand. Stays independent. |
| Duininck Heavy Civil (Midwest) | Construction & Materials | MN / SD | Core | Being unified under single Duininck brand |
| Duininck Heavy Civil (Texas) | Construction & Materials | TX | Core | Being unified. Was managed separately. |
| Duininck Concrete (being absorbed) | Construction & Materials | Willmar, MN | Merging | CONFIRMED: Brand being eliminated. Folding into unified Duininck. |
| Duininck Golf | Construction & Materials | Delano, MN & Atlanta, GA | Specialty / Niche | Keeps niche positioning. Different audience (architects vs. GCs). Went dark on social. |
| Hart Ranch Golf Club | Real Estate | Rapid City, SD | Premium | #1 Public Golf Course in South Dakota (Golfweek). Independent brand. |
| Hart Ranch / Duininck Development | Real Estate | SD & Willmar, MN | Growth | Independent brand. Ranchette development, campground, business park. |

### Key Structural Finding
Prinsco (~$95.7M estimated revenue via public records) is likely the largest revenue generator in the portfolio, yet shows zero visual or brand connection to the parent company. Hart Ranch operates the same way. A customer of Prinsco pipe in Iowa has no idea they're buying from the same family that builds golf courses for Hazeltine and roads for MnDOT. This is both a brand architecture challenge and a massive untold story.

## The "Invisible" Findings

These are the most strategically valuable observations, the things nobody inside the company can see because they're too close:

1. **The prestige gap**: Duininck Golf has worked on Hazeltine National, Erin Hills, TPC venues. This is world-class pedigree buried on a separate site with minimal social presence. A golf course architect searching for builders would likely never find this.

2. **The family story is invisible**: 100 years, 4 generations, Depression-era bankruptcy and rebuild, faith-based values. None of this is told prominently or compellingly across the digital presence.

3. **Six brands, zero connective tissue**: A customer of Prinsco pipe, a golfer at Hart Ranch, and a county engineer hiring Duininck for road work would never know they're all connected to the same family.

4. **The centennial is not being used**: The single biggest brand credibility asset (100 years of continuous family operation) gets no prominent digital treatment. By July 25, this should be the lead message everywhere.

5. **The recruiting story is stronger than the recruiting materials**: The wellbeing framework (Career, Family/Social, Finances, Health, Community, Spiritual) is a genuinely differentiated employer value proposition, but the job postings and career pages read like standard construction company listings.
