## Cross-Property Visual Consistency

### Key Finding: 6 Properties, 6 Different Treatments
The Duininck portfolio has significant visual drift. Each web property uses a slightly different combination of colors, fonts, photography style, and logo treatments. To an outsider, these look like 6 different companies, not one family.

**Visual drift areas identified:**
- Color usage inconsistencies across sub-brand sites
- Different photography styles (some stock, some custom, varying quality)
- Logo treatments vary in placement, size, and surrounding whitespace
- Footer designs, navigation patterns, and CTA styles differ per property
- Some properties use brand colors correctly; others have drift into off-palette shades

### Logo Status
- Brand unification in progress
- Concrete brand logo being retired
- Golf retains niche visual treatment

## Photography Style Observations

### What Nicole Confirmed
- No talking heads (workers are blue-collar and camera-shy)
- Graphics underperform on social ("two likes")
- Carousels outperform static images
- Video of workers DOING the work (with voiceover) is acceptable
- Golf beauty shots lag construction by a full year (growing season)

### What We See on Current Properties
- Mix of stock and custom photography
- Equipment and project photos vary widely in quality
- Golf portfolio has strong project photography but limited web presence
- Heavy civil photography tends toward generic construction imagery
- Drone footage appears underutilized

### Photography Opportunity
The constraint (no talking heads) is actually a brand advantage. When the work IS the protagonist, the content feels more authentic than competitors using posed employee photos. Cinematic b-roll of equipment, drone footage of project scale, time-lapse of construction phases, and close-up "hands at work" shots can create a distinctive visual language that no competitor matches.

## Recommendations (Preliminary)

1. **Establish a unified color system**: Primary (Teal #004F71), secondary (Orange #FE5000), with a defined palette of neutrals and supporting colors. All properties adopt this immediately.

2. **Standardize typography**: Pick one font stack for the entire brand. Roboto is currently in use; consider whether it carries enough character for a 100-year brand or if something with more heritage/craft would serve better.

3. **Create a photography style guide**: Codify the "no talking heads, work as protagonist" approach. Define shot lists for project types, equipment, seasonal capture, and drone work.

4. **Design the endorsement treatment**: "A Duininck Company" lockup for Golf. Clear guidelines on when and how to show the parent brand connection.

5. **Kill the Concrete brand visually**: Identify every remaining Concrete brand touchpoint and create a sunset checklist.
