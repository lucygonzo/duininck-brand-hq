> Priority: HIGH (influence project shortlists and specifications)
> Last Updated: March 2026
> Status: Research Complete

---

## Who They Are
Professional/technical consultants who design projects, write specifications, and manage construction on behalf of public agencies. In Minnesota, MnDOT maintains a formal pre-qualification system for consulting firms. These engineers don't select contractors directly (that's the bid process), but they influence outcomes through specifications, recommendations, and performance evaluations.

---

## How the MnDOT Consultant System Works
- MnDOT maintains a pre-qualification program for Professional/Technical consultants
- Each project has an "Official List" identifying eligible firms
- Firms must be pre-qualified in specific "work types" to submit for projects
- MnDOT publishes upcoming P/T projects regularly (dot.state.mn.us/consult/notices.html)
- Key contact: MnDOT Liaison to P/T Consultants, Laurie Ryan (651-245-8368)

---

## How They Influence Contractor Selection

| Mechanism | How It Works |
|-----------|-------------|
| Specification writing | Specs that favor certain capabilities can advantage contractors with matching experience |
| Negotiated work recommendations | On design-build or negotiated projects, engineer recommendations directly influence selection |
| Performance evaluation | Post-project evaluations affect contractor reputation for future work |
| County engineer relationships | MN has 87 counties, each with an engineer who influences local project decisions |
| Pre-bid conference input | Engineers set the tone for how a project is perceived and who's "expected" to bid |

## What Moves This Audience
Engineers care about competence, reliability, and collaboration. They want contractors who:
- Solve problems instead of creating them
- Build what's drawn instead of cutting corners
- Communicate proactively when issues arise
- Have documented experience on similar project types
- Maintain strong safety programs

Duininck's 100-year track record IS the proof point. A well-documented project portfolio (specs met, on time, on budget, no claims) is the most powerful marketing asset for engineers.
