## Who They Are
Blue-collar operators, skilled trades, equipment operators, and laborers. Primarily seasonal hires needed every construction season (April-November). Concentrated in MN, SD, TX. Median age skews younger for seasonal roles. Many have CDLs or are willing to get one. They have smartphones but don't use LinkedIn.

### The "Marcus" Persona
Marcus, 25, equipment operator in the Willmar MN area. Currently works for a competitor. Has a wife and 1-year-old. Scrolls Facebook and Instagram. Uses Indeed for job searches. Doesn't have LinkedIn. His wife googles company reviews before he applies anywhere.

## Where Duininck Loses This Audience
1. **Discovery**: Job postings read like every other construction company. No mention of the things that make Duininck different.
2. **The Glassdoor check**: Only 9 reviews. When a recruit's spouse googles "Duininck reviews," they see almost nothing. Ames has 114, Knife River 141.
3. **The "so what?" test**: $27-$35/hr is competitive but not dominant. Benefits are standard. Nothing makes a 25-year-old choose Duininck over Knife River.
4. **Scale perception**: Knife River has 674 open positions. Duininck has 25. Volume signals "real opportunity."

## Where Duininck Could Win This Audience
1. **The "whole person" positioning**: No competitor uses this language. The 6-dimension wellbeing framework is genuinely unique.
2. **The 100-year proof**: "Join a company that's been building for 100 years and treats you like a whole person."
3. **The spouse test**: Wellbeing framework speaks to spouses: "They care about your family, your finances, your health."
4. **Glassdoor activation**: Target 50+ reviews within 6 months. Employees already say positive things.
5. **Centennial as recruiting anchor**: "Come build the next 100 years with us."

## Trade School & Vocational Pipeline

### Programs Within Duininck's Recruiting Radius

**Ridgewater College (Willmar, MN)** [Literally in Duininck's backyard]
- Construction Trades program with multiple pathways
- Heavy equipment classes: forklift, skid loader, mobile crane certification, CDL prep
- Basic Construction Skills: OSHA 10-hour safety card, hand/power tools, construction math
- Construction Craft Laborer: carpentry, masonry, ironworking, electrical, welding, heavy equipment
- Customized Training Solutions for employers (Duininck could sponsor a cohort)

**Central Lakes College (Brainerd/Staples, MN)**
- Heavy Equipment Operation & Maintenance (1-year and 2-year tracks)
- Graduates earn: OSHA 10-hour card, Class A CDL, CPR/AED cert, MN aggregate production cert

**Dakota County Technical College (Rosemount, MN)**
- Heavy Construction Equipment Technology program, year-round

**Minnesota State Commercial Driver Academy**
- Standardized CDL training across three campuses, designed for employer partnerships

**MnDOT Youth Pipeline**
- Operating Engineers Local 49 Pathway for high school students
- Heavy equipment operation while earning high school credit

### Pipeline Recommendations
1. "Duininck Scholars" at Ridgewater College: tuition assistance for seasonal commitment
2. Central Lakes College career fairs and site visit program
3. Formalize seasonal-to-permanent apprenticeship pathways
4. Connect with Local 49/MnDOT youth pathway for early brand awareness
5. CDL sponsorship to match Knife River's paid CDL training
6. Centennial campaign targeting graduating students
