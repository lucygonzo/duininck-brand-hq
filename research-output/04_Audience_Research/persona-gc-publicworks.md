## Who They Are
County road department managers, municipal engineers, state DOT project managers (especially MnDOT), and general contractors who bid and contract heavy civil work. Decision-makers who evaluate contractors through formal bid processes. Nicole conducted customer interviews with GCs and public works during brand unification research.

---

## How They Find Contractors

### Formal Bid Process (Government/Public Works)
- MnDOT uses a formal bid letting process. Projects posted on dot.state.mn.us/bidlet/
- Contractors must register with MN Secretary of State and provide 5% proposal guarantee
- Contracts awarded to lowest responsible and responsive bidder
- Reputation, safety record, and past performance matter for "responsible" determination
- Bid results are public (dot.state.mn.us/bidlet/abstract.html), meaning competitors see every win/loss

### Private Sector (General Contractors)
- Relationship-driven. GCs work with contractors they know and trust.
- Prequalification lists matter. Being on the right lists means getting invited to bid.
- Trade associations (AGC, NAPA) create networking and visibility.
- Project references and case studies influence shortlisting.

## Key Finding
This audience doesn't "choose" Duininck from a website. They encounter Duininck through bid lists, past project experience, and industry reputation. Brand work for this audience is about REINFORCEMENT, not discovery. When a county engineer sees the Duininck name on a bid, we want instant association with "100 years, solid reputation, safe crews, they always deliver."

### That association is built through:
1. Consistent trade publication presence (ENR rankings, NAPA awards)
2. Award submissions and recognition marketing
3. Case study documentation (before/after, on-time/on-budget)
4. Safety record marketing (incident rates, zero-injury milestones)
5. Community reputation in operating markets
6. Centennial proof point: "We've been delivering for 100 years."
