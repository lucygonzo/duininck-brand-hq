## Who They Are
Course architects (members of ASGCA), private club boards, resort operators, and municipal golf authorities. A small, tight-knit, prestige-driven community where reputation travels fast and portfolio quality is everything.

Nicole confirmed: "Golf still has a bit of a niche because of the architects that come to that site versus general contractors and public works."

## How They Find Builders

| Channel | Details |
|---------|---------|
| ASGCA (American Society of Golf Course Architects) | Primary professional association. Maintains Industry Providers database. |
| GCBAA (Golf Course Builders Association of America) | Trade association for golf builders. Membership signals legitimacy. |
| Architect relationships | "Having experience with similar projects and a relationship with the architect on past projects is always preferred." Relationships drive most selection. |
| Golf Course Architecture magazine | Industry publication. Project profiles influence architect perception. |
| Instagram | Architects and club boards evaluate project quality visually. Portfolio is essential. |
| Conference networking | ASGCA annual meetings, Golf Industry Show, GCBAA events |

## What Moves This Audience
1. **Portfolio quality**: Before/after photos of restorations. The names of original architects (Ross, Raynor, Tillinghast = instant credibility).
2. **Architect relationships**: Repeat work with respected architects signals trust.
3. **Craft attention**: Drainage, shaping, grow-in, turf quality. Technical craft matters as much as visual result.
4. **The "one year later" story**: Golf courses need 12+ months after construction to reach beauty. Time-lapse stories (construction > grow-in > maturity) are the most compelling content type.
5. **Heritage**: Being part of a 100-year family company that also builds highways and water systems signals financial stability, equipment access, and operational depth that pure golf builders can't match.
