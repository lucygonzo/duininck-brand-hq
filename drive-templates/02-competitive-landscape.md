## Key Insight Before You Start
Duininck's real competitive advantage isn't operational — it's the 100-year family story, the values culture, and the "whole person" employee approach. No competitor can replicate a century. The competitive analysis should validate this wedge, not just compare capabilities.

## Claude Prompts for Research

### Prompt 1: Competitor Deep Dive
```
Research [COMPETITOR NAME] as a competitor to Duininck Companies in the [SECTOR] space. I need:

1. Company overview: size, revenue, geography, ownership structure, founding year
2. Brand analysis: tagline, positioning, messaging themes, visual identity quality
3. Digital presence: website quality (1-5), social media followers and posting frequency, content strategy assessment
4. Employer brand: career page messaging, Glassdoor rating, what they offer employees
5. Key differentiators vs. Duininck
6. Weaknesses Duininck can exploit

Context: Duininck is a 100-year-old, 4th-generation family business with 700+ employees in MN/TX/GA/SD. Their wedge is the "whole person" employee approach, vertically integrated capabilities, and century of family trust.

Be specific. Use real data. Don't hedge.
```

### Prompt 2: Employer Brand Landscape
```
Analyze the employer brand landscape for heavy civil construction companies in the Midwest United States. I need to understand:

1. How are these companies positioning themselves to attract blue-collar workers?
2. What benefits and culture messaging are they using?
3. Which companies have the strongest career pages and recruiting content?
4. Is ANY company in this space using a "whole person" wellbeing approach (covering career, family, health, community, spiritual dimensions)?

Compare: Ames Construction, C.S. McCrossan, Knife River, Aggregate Industries, and Duininck Companies.

I need to validate whether Duininck's 6-dimension wellbeing framework is truly unique in this market.
```
