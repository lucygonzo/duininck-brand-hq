## The Untouchable Recruiting Wedge

### The Problem
Duininck hires seasonal blue-collar workers every construction season. Competition for talent is fierce. Traditional construction recruiting says: "competitive pay, good benefits, join our team."

### The Duininck Advantage
No construction company in the Midwest talks about employees the way Duininck does. Their 6-dimension wellbeing framework treats every worker as a **whole person** — not headcount. This is the recruiting moat:

| Dimension | What It Means | Competitor Equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| **Career** | Growth paths, skills development, equipment training | Most offer this |
| **Family/Social** | Support for family life, not just work life | Rare in construction |
| **Finances** | Financial wellness beyond just a paycheck | Uncommon |
| **Health** | Physical + mental health support for physical laborers | Basic health insurance only |
| **Community** | Connection to the communities where they build | Almost never messaged |
| **Spiritual** | Faith-based values that are authentic, not performative | Unique to Duininck |

**Hypothesis to validate:** No major construction competitor in MN/TX/SD/GA is positioning with a whole-person framework. If confirmed, this is ownable white space.

## Content Strategy for Employer Brand

### What Works (from Nicole Discovery)
- Carousels > static graphics ("two likes" on graphics)
- Cinematic b-roll > talking heads (blue-collar won't go on camera)
- Real stories > corporate copy

### Content Types
1. **"Day in the Life" reels** — Follow equipment, not faces. Voiceover by narrator.
2. **Stats-based employee spotlights** — Photo of person WORKING + overlay: name, role, years, projects, miles paved. No interview.
3. **Equipment feature carousels** — "Meet the machine. Meet the operator." (focus on the craft)
4. **"Why I Stay" cards** — Anonymous quotes from retention survey + candid crew photos. "15 years. Because they see the whole me."
5. **Before/after project carousels** — Shows what the team built (pride factor)
6. **Centennial recruiting** — "100 years. Thousands of careers. Yours could be next."

### Blue-Collar Content Capture Rules
From Nicole: "These guys are blue-collar. They don't want to talk to anybody. They're not going to be on camera."

**DO:**
- Film them working (wide shots, action shots, equipment)
- Use drone footage showing scale of their work
- Add voiceover/narrator over b-roll
- Use text overlays on photos (stats, quotes, facts)
- Capture candid moments (lunch break, crew morning huddle)

**DON'T:**
- Ask them to sit for an interview
- Put them in front of a camera with a script
- Create "corporate culture" videos that feel staged
- Use stock photography
